About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in climate science at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology in the department of Earth, Atmosphere and Plantery Sciences with Professor John Marshall. My research lies at the intersection between sea ice, ocean and climate physics. I like to tackle problems with a hierarchy of models ranging from analyitical tools to Global Climate Models (GCMs). A copy of my CV is available here.


Research interests

I am interested in a wide range of topics, including:

  • The sensitivity of the climate to radiative perturbations
  • Multiple equilibria in the climate system and tipping points
  • The role of the Southern Ocean past and present climates
